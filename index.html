My name is Guangliang Liu. My research area is Natural Language Processing, with a current focus on machine morality and computational pragmatics. My research aims to uncover the underlying mechanisms by which statistical machine learning models acquire and utilize pragmatics of morality, and to characterize their generalization behavior from both statistical and linguistic perspectives.

I am extremely fortunate to be advised by Dr. Kristen Marie Johnson. I also collaborate closely with Dr. Rongrong Wang at Michigan State University and Dr. Zoe Xi Chen at Nanyang Technological University.

Reearch Interests. My ultimate goal is to equip machines with linguistic competence and social cognizance. I have broad interests in psycholinguistics, statistical learning theory and cognitive science. I am currently interested in several interrelated research problems:
How can we enable machines to emulate human moral decision-making through analogical reasoning?
How can pragmatic inference be modeled computationally to address diverse moral issues?
How can multi-step semantic inference approximate the process of pragmatic reasoning?
How can we develop a computational framework to support meta-pragmatic inference?
